## SECTION 1 — COMPANY CONTEXT (use in every prompt)

**Company:** Bellwood Ventures Ltd  
**Type:** UK direct-to-vendor cash property buyer + deal sourcer  
**Stage:** Pre-launch, April 2026  
**Founder context:** CPO/product leader background; highly analytical, data-driven, dyslexic-friendly formatting preferred (bullets, bold key points, short sentences, no walls of text)

### The Core Thesis

Estate agents are Bellwood's primary distribution channel. They already have distressed sellers. They face real pain every week — chain breaks, probate, problem properties — and currently have no specialist partner who makes them look good, keeps their commission, and handles the compliance. Bellwood flips this: agents actively refer because Bellwood makes them money, protects them legally, and never lets them down.

### The Business Model — Three Revenue Streams

**Stream 1 — Principal buy-to-resell (primary)**
- Buy direct from motivated sellers at 71–80% of open-market value (OMV)
- Light refurbishment where needed
- Resell at 95–107% OMV
- SDLT Property Trader Relief (Schedule 6A FA 2003) = zero stamp duty on qualifying probate and chain-break purchases
- Target net profit: £11k–£29k per deal depending on type

**Stream 2 — Deal sourcing for investors (secondary)**
- Source qualifying below-market-value deals for a network of guaranteed investors
- Package as a compliant investor deal pack (NAPSA-aligned, RICS valuation, COI declaration)
- Charge sourcing fee: £5,000–£7,000 per deal
- CRITICAL RULE: a deal cannot be in both Stream 1 and Stream 2 simultaneously — strict conflict-of-interest policy applies

**Stream 3 — Auction backstop (tertiary)**
- Partner with UK auction houses (SDL, Auction House UK, Clive Emson, Pugh, Edward Mellor)
- When lots go unsold, Bellwood buys within 24 hours under auction conditions
- London clearance rates fell to 36% in April 2026 — significant unsold lot volume nationally
- Pre-qualified sellers, legal pack already prepared, no marketing cost

|---|---|---|---|
| Buy-to-resell (net) | £149,000 | £423,000 | £955,000 |

Deals: 8 (Y1) → 18 (Y2) → 32 (Y3) across all streams. Capital is not a constraint — Bellwood has a network of guaranteed investors.

### The Three Deal Types Bellwood Buys

| Deal Type | Situation | SDLT | Purchase % OMV | Why auctions/open market fail |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Type A — Probate** | Executor needs to sell; grant may not yet be issued; IHT deadline; multiple beneficiaries | Zero (Schedule 6A) | 71–76% | Can't legally exchange pre-grant; legal pack complexity; thin bidder pool |
| **Type B — Chain-break** | Buyer up the chain pulls out; seller at risk of losing onward purchase; must complete in days | Zero (chain-break relief) | 75–80% | Open market takes 3–6 months; mortgage buyers carry same chain risk |
| **Type C — Problem property** | Knotweed, cladding, short lease, non-standard construction, structural defect | Full rate (budget accordingly) | 68–72% | Lenders won't lend; auction bidder pool is 2–3 specialists |

### What Makes Bellwood Different (the moat)

1. **Written no-re-trade guarantee** — signed offer is final price. No survey price cuts. Most complained-about behaviour in the sector.
2. **Seller Disclosure Form provided** — legally required under TPO Code + DMCC Act 2025; no competitor provides it. Removes agent's biggest legal risk.
3. **AML handled on seller** — Thirdfort KYC receipt given to agent; removes their HMRC exposure.
4. **Agent's existing agency fee covered** — Bellwood pays 1.4% OMV break cost so agent never looks like they're stealing the client.
5. **Auction backstop positioning** — Bellwood is not competing with auctions; it's the backstop for failed lots and pre-grant probate.

## SECTION 3 — AGENT DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY (use for: sales agent, outreach agent, CRM agent)

### Who to Target

**Best agent type:** Independent branch manager or director, 3–10 offices, 50–150 sales/year. They decide on the day. They feel the pain personally when a deal falls through. They are not protected by a corporate compliance team that will slow referrals.

**Avoid initially:** Corporate chains (Connells, Countrywide, Foxtons) — compliance bureaucracy slows everything. Purplebricks/online-only — no relationship to leverage.

**Priority agent profiles:**
- Agents already working with LPA receivers, probate solicitors, or auction houses — pre-wired for Bellwood
- Agents with high-volume probate pipeline (check if they have "probate" in their listing descriptions)
- Agents in knotweed hotspots (Bristol, Merseyside, Manchester, Cardiff, Swansea) for Type C problem property deals
- Agents who have publicly complained about cash buyers on LinkedIn or Property118

### The Outreach Message (adapt per agent)

> "I'm building Bellwood Ventures — a specialist cash buyer for the deals you can't move: probate, chain-breaks, knotweed, failed auctions. Unlike most cash buyers, we never re-trade, we pay your commission in full, and we give you a legally compliant disclosure pack that protects you under the new DMCC Act. I'd love 30 minutes to understand what deals cost you most — no pitch, just research. Can we speak this week?"

### The Five Deal Triggers to Plant in Agent's Mind

Train every agent partner to call Bellwood when they see ANY of these:

1. 🔴 **Chain breaks** — buyer up the chain pulls out; seller about to lose onward purchase
2. ⚫ **Probate estate** — grant not yet issued; IHT clock running; multiple beneficiaries
3. 🟠 **Problem property** — knotweed, cladding EWS1, short lease, non-standard construction
4. 🟡 **Failed auction lot** — went to auction, didn't sell; seller demoralised, legal pack exists
5. 🔵 **Distressed / divorce / relocation** — seller needs certainty and privacy over top price

### What Agents Earn (always state this explicitly)

- **Referral fee:** 1.5% + VAT of purchase price, paid on completion
- **Resale instruction (Type C):** After Bellwood remediates/improves the property, agent is instructed for the resale — earns their normal selling fee again
- **Seller break cost covered:** Bellwood pays the seller's existing agent fee (up to 1.4% OMV)
- **Compliance cover:** AML KYC receipt + Seller Disclosure Form provided — protects agent under DMCC Act 2025

### Agent Discovery Interview Script (8–10 agents before building anything)

Use Jobs-to-Be-Done switch interview methodology. Key questions:

1. "Think about the last time a sale fell through. Walk me through that deal."
2. "What did you do when you realised it was at risk? Who did you call?"
3. "Do you currently work with any cash buyers? Walk me through the last referral."
4. "What annoyed you most about how that cash buyer operated?"
5. "If I gave you a tool to generate an instant cash offer while still in the seller's living room — would you use that?"
6. "What would make you think of a cash buyer FIRST, before trying anything else?"
7. Forced rank: "Which of these would you cut first: no-re-trade guarantee / commission paid / AML offload / 2-hour offer / dashboard / resale instruction?"
8. "What one thing would make you confident enough to recommend a cash buyer to a colleague?"

## SECTION 4 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST RULES (use for: compliance agent, legal agent, operations agent)

### The Core Problem

Bellwood operates two revenue streams that create genuine conflicts of interest:
- **Stream 1:** Bellwood buys property as principal (benefits from buying cheaply)
- **Stream 2:** Bellwood sources deals for investors (investor pays Bellwood a fee; Bellwood should act in investor's interest)

These interests can conflict. The rules below are mandatory. Non-compliance = deal cancelled.

### The Six Non-Negotiable Rules

**RULE 1 — No cross-stream allocation**
A deal identified for Stream 2 (sourcing for investor) may NEVER be purchased by Bellwood for Stream 1 (own account) unless the investor formally declines in writing. Written audit trail required.

**RULE 2 — Agent referral fee disclosed to seller in writing, always**
Every referral fee must be named on the Seller Disclosure Form before the seller signs anything. Amount, recipient, timing. No verbal-only disclosure. This is now a DMCC Act 2025 legal requirement.

**RULE 3 — Independent valuation on every sourced deal**
Every investor deal pack must include an independent RICS valuation OR 3 comparable Rightmove/Zoopla sold prices (within 0.5 miles, within 12 months). No exceptions. This protects Bellwood from "you sold me an overpriced deal" claims.

**RULE 4 — AML on every seller and every investor**
Thirdfort KYC check before any offer to seller. Thirdfort + AML Risk Assessment form before any sourcing fee received from investor. HMRC AML supervision registration must be in place before Deal 1.

**RULE 5 — No conditional selling**
Bellwood must never instruct an agent to favour buyers who use Bellwood-recommended solicitors or financial advisers. Post-BBC Panorama CLC review — this is the exact behaviour being investigated.

**RULE 6 — Agent double-earn fully disclosed upfront**
If Bellwood will instruct the referring agent to sell the property post-purchase, this must be disclosed to the original seller in the Seller Disclosure Form — not after the fact.

### The Vulnerable Seller Protocol

The biggest reputational risk in cash property buying is press coverage of exploited distressed sellers. Mandatory protections:
- Never buy from a seller who appears to lack mental capacity without GP or solicitor certificate
- Never give less than 7 days to decide (use 14-day offer expiry as standard)
- Always confirm in writing that seller has been advised to seek independent legal advice
- Never use language that creates artificial urgency ("this offer expires tonight")

## SECTION 5 — PRODUCT ROADMAP (use for: product agent, tech agent, build agent)

### The Build Cost Reality in 2026

The old framing — "don't build a portal until you have 5 deals" — was based on a 2023 cost model where a working agent portal cost £20k–50k and 2–3 months of engineering time. That's dead. With Claude Code, founders are shipping working MVPs in **8 hours of actual build time**. AI MVP build cycles have compressed from 2–3 months to 1–7 days, at near-zero marginal cost (£0–100/month in subscriptions vs £50k+ traditional dev).

For a founder with a product and AI background, the calculus is entirely different. **Build everything. Build it now. Show up to the first agent meeting with a working demo — it's your best sales tool.**

The one thing that doesn't change: do the 8–10 discovery interviews first. Not because building is expensive — because **building the wrong thing is now even more obviously wasteful when it only takes a weekend**. Interviews first, build second. You'll know exactly what to put on the offer calculator and what agents actually need to see.

### What to Build — Revised Sequencing

| Phase | Trigger | What to build | Stack | Est. build time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** — before first agent meeting | Right now | Agent portal v1 (deal tracker, commission log, compliance log); Instant offer calculator; Seller Disclosure Form generator (auto-populates PDF); AML/KYC tracker (Thirdfort integration) | Claude Code + Supabase + Next.js | 3–4 days |
| **Week 2** — after discovery interviews | Post-interview insights | Refine offer calculator parameters; add deal type logic (A/B/C); tweak portal views based on what agents said they need | Claude Code | 1 day |
| **Month 2** — after 3 completed deals | Real usage data | Agent portal v2: email/SMS notifications on deal status changes; referral league table; mobile-responsive views | Claude Code | 2–3 days |
| **Month 4** — after 10 completed deals | Training data exists | AI offer generation: input address → instant indicative offer within defined parameters, flagged to founder for approval before sending | Claude API + Rightmove/Zoopla data feed | 1 week |
| **Year 2** — volume justifies it | CRM usage data from agents | Alto/Reapit CRM integration (build for whichever CRM your top agents actually use); HMRC probate registry API; investor secondary market portal | Claude Code + API partners | Sprint-based |

### What to Build in Week 1 (Detailed Brief for Claude Code)

**1. Agent Portal (v1)**
- Supabase backend (auth + database); Next.js frontend
- Tables: deals, agent partners, compliance log, payments
- Views: per-agent deal status, live commission tracker, referral count
- Key insight: showing up with this at the first agent meeting is worth more than any pitch deck

**2. Instant Offer Calculator**
- Agent inputs: address, OMV estimate, deal type (A/B/C/backstop), urgency level
- Output: indicative offer range, deal type classification, expected timeline, SDLT position flag
- Agent can run this in the seller's living room before calling Bellwood — this is the most powerful referral trigger in the whole product

**3. Seller Disclosure Form Generator**
- Agent inputs deal details → system auto-populates the legally required disclosure form
- PDF output, ready to print or email on the spot
- Removes friction of manually completing Tab 3 of the workbook on every deal

**4. AML / KYC Tracker**
- Thirdfort API integration
- Logs check date, reference, result per seller and per investor
- Auto-generates compliance receipt for referring agent (their legal protection)

### What Still Shouldn't Be Built Early

- **AI pricing engine** — needs 10+ completed deals as training data before it's meaningful
- **CRM integrations** — find out which CRM your top agent partners actually use before building the integration
- **Mobile app** — agents use desktop/email for this relationship type; mobile-responsive web is sufficient

## SECTION 6 — SPECIFIC AI AGENT TASK BRIEFS

### Agent Brief A: Content + LinkedIn Agent

**Your role:** Create LinkedIn posts, articles, and email sequences for Bellwood Ventures.

**Tone:** Authoritative, specific, founder-voiced. Short sentences. Bold punchlines. Always lead with a statistic or a provocative statement. Never fluffy. The audience is estate agents — they are busy, sceptical, and have been burned by bad cash buyers before.

**Key messages to rotate:**
1. "26% of your sales will fall through this year. Here's what to do when it happens." (chain-break pain)
2. "Every cash buyer we've ever met will re-trade on you. Here's the only way to prevent it." (written guarantee)
3. "BBC Panorama came for Connells. Here's the disclosure form that protects you." (compliance/fear)
4. "Auctions don't solve probate. Here's why." (education — pre-grant problem)
5. "What your seller's Japanese knotweed actually means for your commission." (Type C problem)

**Content to avoid:** Generic "we're different" language. Unsourced claims. Anything that sounds like an advert. Self-congratulation.

**Evergreen angles:** IHT deadlines (6-month rule), chain-break statistics, knotweed hotspot maps, DMCC Act implications for agents, TPO complaint trends.

### Agent Brief B: Legal / Compliance Agent

**Your role:** Review, draft, and quality-check Bellwood's legal documents.

**Key documents to maintain:**
- Seller Disclosure Form (must comply with: TPO Code, DMCC Act 2025, CPR 2008, EAA 1979 s.18/21)
- Investor Deal Pack (must comply with: NAPSA guidelines, Consumer Protection Act, MLR 2017)
- COI Policy (six rules — see Section 4)
- SDLT relief checklist (post-Mudan v HMRC  — every deal claiming relief needs its own assessment)
- AML Risk Assessment form (HMRC MLR 2017 format)

**Red flags to always check:**
- Any deal where Bellwood's purchase price is not disclosed to seller in writing before exchange
- Any sourced deal where Bellwood could benefit from buying it itself
- Any suggestion of "conditional selling" (directing seller to specific solicitors or financial advisers)
- Any SDLT relief claim on a property that was not the deceased's main residence in the last 2 years
- Any deal with a seller who may lack capacity (elderly, distressed, under duress)

### Agent Brief C: Research Agent

**Your role:** Monitor market intelligence and flag relevant developments.

**Watch list (check weekly):**
- QuickMoveNow fall-through tracker (monthly data)
- SDL Property Auctions unsold lots page
- Auction House UK unsold lots page
- HMRC AML enforcement notices (property sector fines)
- TPO annual report / complaints data (published annually)
- CLC referral fee review updates (triggered by BBC Panorama July 2025)
- HMRC SDLT guidance updates (post-Mudan v HMRC )
- UK Finance / Nationwide / Halifax house price data (regional)
- Property118, LandlordZone, Reddit r/UKPropertyInvesting — agent sentiment on cash buyers
- LinkedIn: search "chain break" + "estate agent" + "cash buyer" — surface agent pain stories

**Output format:** Weekly bulletin. Bullets only. Bold anything requiring founder action. Flag anything that changes the legal compliance position immediately.

### Agent Brief D: CRM / Deal Tracking Agent

**Your role:** Maintain Bellwood's deal pipeline, agent partner database, and compliance log in Airtable.

**Deal tracker fields (every deal):**
- Deal ID, deal type (A/B/C/Backstop), address, agent partner, seller name
- OMV (source), offer price, offer date, offer expiry
- SDLT position (relief claimed Y/N, tax opinion received Y/N)
- KYC status (Thirdfort check date, reference)
- Seller Disclosure Form signed (date)
- Exchange date, completion date
- Stream assignment (Stream 1 — Bellwood buys / Stream 2 — sourced for investor)
- Investor name (if Stream 2), sourcing fee amount, fee paid date
- Referring agent fee amount, fee paid date
- Net profit (actual vs forecast)
- Compliance notes (any red flags or unusual circumstances)

**Agent partner fields:**
- Agency name, branch address, contact name, email, phone, LinkedIn
- Region, deal types they handle, referral fee agreement signed (Y/N)
- Deals referred (count), deals completed, total fees paid
- Last contact date, next follow-up date
- Notes (from discovery interview, preferences, concerns)

### Agent Brief E: Financial Modelling Agent

**Your role:** Maintain and update Bellwood's financial model.

**Key assumptions to update monthly:**
- Average purchase price by region (source: Nationwide/Halifax HPI)
- Bridging finance rate (source: Westminster Finance / Bridging Trends quarterly)
- Conveyancing cost (source: PropertySolvers annual survey)
- Average deal cycle time (Bellwood actuals, once deals start)
- Sourcing fee market rate (source: PropSourcer, NAPSA, competitor benchmarking)

**Per-deal P&L template (Stream 1):**
Purchase price + SDLT (zero if relief; full rate at relevant threshold if not) + conveyancing (both sides) + bridging (rate × LTV × months) + bridging arrangement fee + RICS survey + refurbishment + void costs + agent resale fee + referring agent fee + seller existing agency fee + legal/AML costs = total costs. Resale proceeds - total costs = net profit.

**Revenue forecast update trigger:** Any time average deal count changes by more than ±2 from forecast, or any time SDLT relief is challenged by HMRC, update the model and flag to founder.
