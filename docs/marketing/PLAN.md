## §1 The frame — two customers, one brand

Bellwood markets to **two distinct customers** that need entirely different
content, channels, and tone. Same brand, same promises, different surface
area.

| | **Estate agents** | **Vendors (sellers)** |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | Repeat (~5 viable moments/year) | One-time |
| State of mind | Time-pressured but professional | Distressed, often embarrassed |
| What they search for | Trust + reliability + revenue | Help + speed + privacy |
| Time horizon | Weeks/months (relationship) | Hours (panic) |
| Where they look | LinkedIn, email, IG (lurking), word-of-mouth | Google search, Meta retargeting, solicitors |
| Trust threshold | Medium (peer-to-peer) | Very high (fear of being ripped off) |
| Volume potential | 200/yr long-term | 50–80/yr long-term |
| Cost per acquisition | High — pays back many times | High — pays back once, bigger margin |
| Approval rule | Held draft, CEO approves, auto-send permitted | **Always held, CEO approves, never auto-send** |

**Key insight:** vendors don't follow Instagram. They Google in private at
11pm, on a phone, after a difficult day. Agents don't read distress-blog
posts; they network on LinkedIn and reply to peer-style emails.

**Strategic principle:** authenticity over volume. *"We won't buy any home"*
is positioning, not a tagline. Volume claims look desperate; selectivity
reads as confidence. This rule shapes every piece of marketing.

## §2 Brand voice — the one rule that binds both audiences

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Adjectives, superlatives | Numbers + specifics |
| "Get cash today!" | "Some questions about how this works" |
| "We buy any house" | "Here's what we won't buy" |
| Stock smiling families | Anonymised real transactions |
| Countdown-timer urgency | The 72-hour offer lock is enough |
| "UK's most trusted" | "We're a small UK firm. Here are our credentials." |
| Jargon | Plain English |

The voice that emerges: **professional, specific, slightly dry,
unapologetic about being selective.** Closer to a chartered surveyor than
a property influencer. Agents read it as confidence; vendors read it as
trustworthy. Designer + Counsel review every page to catch tone drift.

## §3 Vendor segments (5)

In priority order for Bellwood's economics:

### 3.1 Probate executors (highest priority)

- **Why:** HMRC IHT clock = real time pressure. Empty property bleeding council
  tax. Underserved. Low emotional attachment to maximum price.
- **Channel:** Google search, solicitor referrals, local press in target
  postcodes
- **Landing:** `/sell/probate`
- **Content:** probate-specific blog posts, anonymised executor case studies

### 3.2 Chain break vendors going direct

- **Why:** Most go via their agent (we capture them via the agent flow). A
  slice search direct out of frustration.
- **Channel:** Google search ads on chain-break terms
- **Landing:** `/save-the-sale`
- **Content:** "what happens when your buyer pulls out" guide

### 3.3 Mortgage-refused / survey down-valued vendors

- **Why:** Mostly the agent's job. Some search direct, especially weekends.
- **Channel:** Google search, lower volume
- **Landing:** `/save-the-sale`
- **Content:** rolled into chain-break library

### 3.4 Distressed sellers (divorce, repossession, financial)

- **Why:** Highest sensitivity. Done badly = vulture. Done well = trusted.
- **Channel:** Carefully — Google search with empathetic copy
- **Landing:** `/sell/distress` — must signpost StepChange, Citizens Advice
- **Content:** **don't run paid here until Counsel has reviewed every word.**
  Reputational damage is permanent.

### 3.5 Problem property vendors

- **Why:** Niche, highest margin. Knotweed, short lease, cladding, structural.
- **Channel:** Long-tail SEO does most of the work
- **Landing:** `/sell/problem-property`
- **Content:** frank — what we'll buy, what we won't, typical discount

## §4 Channel mix

### 4.1 Estate agents

| Channel | Use | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (org + founder personal accounts)** | Education, case studies, organic reach to peers | 5×/week target |
| **Email outreach** | Targeted to firms surfaced by Monday prospecting cron | Weekly batch |
| **Instagram (lurking proof)** | "Houses we've offered" / "Houses we've sold" — agents scroll, not engage | 3–5/week (deal-rate dependent) |
| **WhatsApp** | Direct to existing contacts, no broadcast | Per-contact |

### 4.2 Vendors

| Channel | Use | Spend share |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Search** | Distress keywords, postcode-targeted | 50% |
| **Google Performance Max** | Broader intent, retargeting | 15% |
| **SEO content** | Long-tail organic — slower but compounds | (Time, not money) |
| **Meta retargeting** | Visitors who hit `/sell` but didn't submit | 10% |
| **Local press + direct mail** | Probate-specific in target postcodes | 10% |
| **Solicitor partnerships** | Probate + divorce lawyer referrals | 10% |
| **TikTok** | Younger probate executors — test only | 5% |

**Phase 1 budget:** £1,000/month on paid acquisition. Track cost-per-form-
submission tightly. If a segment is <£250 CPA, scale. If >£600 CPA, kill.

**What's NOT in either mix:** X (Twitter) — neither audience is there. No
billboards. No radio.

## §5 The four content streams

### Stream 1 — Instagram: "Houses we've offered"

- **Audience:** mixed (agents lurking, vendors searching, investors)
- **Content:** every signed binding offer becomes a post. Anonymised
  exterior, postcode area only (e.g. "M14"), trigger, offer figure,
  completion days
- **Cadence:** every offer issued (5–10/week at scale, 1–2/week at start)
- **Owner:** Marketer drafts, CEO approves, Liaison posts

### Stream 2 — Instagram: "Houses we've sold"

- **Audience:** investors + agents (resale signal) + vendors (success proof)
- **Content:** every completed resale. Story arc: bought from chain break
  in 14 days → refurbished → resold via [agent name] in N weeks
- **Cadence:** every completion (1–2/month → builds gradually)
- **Owner:** Marketer + Counsel draft, CEO approves, Liaison posts

### Stream 3 — LinkedIn: estate agent education

- **Audience:** branch managers + partners at independent agents in our 16
  target postcodes
- **Content:** chain-break stats, anonymised case studies, "what to do
  before you re-list" angle, tagging working partners (with permission)
- **Cadence:** 5×/week target by Day 90
- **Owner:** Marketer drafts, CEO approves, Liaison or founder posts

### Stream 4 — Vendor SEO + paid

- **Audience:** vendors in distress
- **Content:** distress-question blog library (~30 posts in 90 days),
  anonymised vendor case studies, the 5 trust artefacts (§7), paid ad
  variants per segment
- **Cadence:** 2 SEO posts/week, ad copy review monthly
- **Owner:** Marketer drafts, **Counsel reviews every piece** (CPR 2008,
  NTSELAT, ICO, AML), CEO approves

## §8 The "We won't buy any home" page (anchor asset)

This is the **single most differentiating asset** for both audiences.

**Voice:** confident, specific, slightly contrarian. Disarms the
"vulture cash buyer" suspicion in 30 seconds.

**Structure:**

1. The headline claim — *"Some companies will buy any home. We won't."*
2. The four trigger situations we DO buy (chain break, mortgage refused,
   survey down-valued, probate, problem property)
3. The four situations we DON'T buy + what we recommend instead:
   - Working chain — high-street agent
   - Testing the market for an aspirational price — we can't help
   - In family mediation / social services — legal advice first, not a buyer
   - Where a faster sale leaves untenable debt — StepChange link
4. Two short paragraphs on why selectivity is in the vendor's interest
5. CTA — `/save-the-sale` form for genuine fall-throughs

**SEO bonus:** earns inbound links from money-advice sites (StepChange,
MSE, Citizens Advice). Huge domain authority lift over 12 months.

## §10 Measurement — only 4 numbers per side

### Vendors

1. **Cost per form submission**, by channel + segment. Target: <£250.
2. **Form-to-accepted-offer conversion**. Target: 30%+.
3. **Accepted-to-completed conversion**. Target: 80%+.
4. **Cost per completed deal** all-in. Target: <£1,500 in Phase 1, <£800 in
   Phase 2.

### Agents

1. **Outreach-to-reply rate**, by channel. Target: 15%+ on personalised LinkedIn DM, 5%+ on cold email.
2. **Reply-to-deal-submitted rate**. Target: 20%+.
3. **Submitted-to-completed conversion**. Target: 50%+ (with Phase 1 high
   variance).
4. **Cost per completed deal from agent acquisition**. Target: <£500 in Phase 1.

If Marketer's weekly digest reports these 8 numbers, CEO can run the whole
funnel from a 2-minute Sunday read.

## §11 Compliance + reputational guardrails

These are **non-negotiable**. Counsel enforces.

### Vendor side

- Every blog post + landing page reviewed for Consumer Protection from
  Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 (CPR)
- NTSELAT material information guidance — fee disclosures must be in
  writing before the vendor signs
- ICO + UK GDPR — minimum data, proper retention, opt-out on every email
- ASA / CAP codes — every claim substantiated with linked source
- No emotional manipulation. No countdown timers. No false urgency.
- Distress-targeted ads must signpost free debt advice (StepChange,
  Citizens Advice)

### Agent side

- PECR — opt-out on every email (B2B exemption applies for corporate addresses
  but opt-out still required)
- LinkedIn outreach must be relevant + non-spammy (LinkedIn's TOS + good practice)
- Honest claims — completion rates published quarterly, including failures

### Anonymisation rules (both sides)

For every IG post, case study, or any content referencing a real property:

- Postcode **area** only (M14, not M14 5AB)
- No exterior photo showing street numbers, distinguishing features, neighbour
  identification
- No vendor names, executor names, or solicitor names without explicit
  written consent
- No mention of family circumstance specifics (illness, death cause,
  divorce details)
- 30-day delay between completion and any post that could identify the property
  via timing

## §12 What this plan does NOT do

Things the board may want eventually but aren't in scope here:

- ❌ Billboards / outdoor advertising
- ❌ TV or radio
- ❌ X (Twitter) — neither audience is there meaningfully
- ❌ Pinterest, Reddit, Discord
- ❌ B2C influencer marketing
- ❌ Affiliate programmes
- ❌ White-labelled lead generation
- ❌ Multi-language (UK English-speaking only for Phase 1+2)

If the board wants any of these, raise as a `general` priority `medium`
FounderAction with a P&L proposal. Don't ship without explicit approval.
